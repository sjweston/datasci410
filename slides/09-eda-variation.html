What is EDA?

Know your data before you test it

Before you run a single statistical test, you should know your data well enough to predict what the results will look like.

That‚Äôs what EDA gives you ‚Äî no surprises.

Exploratory vs. confirmatory

Exploratory (EDA) Confirmatory
Goal Discover patterns Test hypotheses
Attitude Curiosity Rigor
Questions Open-ended Pre-specified
Output Visualizations, hunches p-values, conclusions

EDA comes first. You can‚Äôt test a hypothesis you didn‚Äôt notice.

Good EDA means looking before you test

‚ÄúEDA is an attitude, not a technique.‚Äù
‚Äî John Tukey

  • Ask questions
  • Answer them by visualizing data
  • Use what you learn to ask new questions
  • Repeat

There is no single ‚Äúright‚Äù way to do EDA. The goal is understanding.

Outliers

What are outliers?

Values that are unusual compared to the rest of the data. They might be:

  • Real ‚Äî genuinely extreme values (a 95-year-old in a college study)
  • Errors ‚Äî data entry mistakes (age = 999)
  • Interesting ‚Äî worth investigating

Never delete an outlier without understanding it.

Spotting outliers visually

bfi |>
  ggplot(aes(x = age)) +
  geom_histogram(binwidth = 5, fill = "steelblue", color = "white") +
  labs(

Visualizing missingness

Why it matters for EDA

Missing data can bias your exploration:

  • If missingness is related to the variables you‚Äôre studying, your distributions are wrong
  • If certain groups are more likely to have missing data, you might miss important patterns

We‚Äôll cover this in depth in Session 14. For now: always check for missing data before exploring.

The EDA workflow

A systematic approach

  1. Look at the structure ‚Äî glimpse(), summary()
  2. Check for missing data ‚Äî how much? Which variables?
  3. Explore each variable ‚Äî histograms, bar charts, summaries
  4. Look for outliers ‚Äî boxplots, programmatic checks
  5. Ask questions ‚Äî what surprised you? What do you want to know more about?
  6. Repeat ‚Äî EDA is iterative

Key takeaways

  1. EDA is an attitude ‚Äî curiosity, not confirmation
  2. Always visualize ‚Äî summary stats hide patterns
  3. Outliers are information ‚Äî investigate, don‚Äôt delete
  4. Check missing data first ‚Äî it affects everything
  5. Ask questions, then answer them ‚Äî that‚Äôs the loop

The one thing to remember

EDA isn‚Äôt a step you finish. It‚Äôs the habit of looking at your data before you believe anything about it.
