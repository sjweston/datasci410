# Fun Challenge 6: Data Detective

**Due:** Sunday, May 10 at 11:59 PM

You've been given a dataset (`06-data-detective.csv`) from a fictional psychology study on the relationship between sleep, stress, and exam performance in college students.

**Variables:**

- `student_id` — Unique ID
- `hours_sleep` — Average hours of sleep per night (self-reported)
- `stress_level` — Perceived stress score (1--10 scale)
- `exam_score` — Final exam score (0--100)
- `study_hours` — Weekly study hours (self-reported)
- `year` — Year in college (1 = freshman, 2 = sophomore, 3 = junior, 4 = senior)

Something is **weird** about this data. Your job:

1. Make **at least 3 plots** exploring the variables and relationships
2. Identify the **two unusual things** hidden in the data
3. For each anomaly, explain:
   - What you found
   - How you found it (which plot or summary revealed it)
   - What might have gone wrong in the study

**Hint:** Look at distributions, relationships between variables, and whether the data makes sense given what you know about college students.

---

**Submit:** A Quarto document or R script with your plots, code, and written explanations. One submission per team.
