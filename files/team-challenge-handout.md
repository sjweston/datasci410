# The Team Challenge

## How It Works

At the start of the term, you'll be placed on a team of 5--6 students. Throughout the quarter, your team earns points across four categories. A scoreboard is shown at the start of each week so you can track your standing.

The team challenge **does not affect your course grade** --- it's designed to build community and add some friendly competition. The winning team at the end of the term earns a celebration (details TBA).

---

## How Your Team Earns Points

| Category | Points | How you earn it | Frequency |
|----------|--------|----------------|-----------|
| **Pair coding** | 1 | Your team earns a point when all (or nearly all) members submit the in-class exercise | Each class session |
| **Assignments** | 1 | Your team earns a point when all members submit the assignment on time | 8 assignments |
| **Quizzes** | 1 | The top 2 teams by average quiz score each earn a point | 10 quizzes |
| **Fun challenge** | 2 | Complete a short weekly team challenge outside of class | 10 weeks |

### A few things to know

- **"Nearly all" means all but one.** If one teammate is absent, it won't tank your team's pair coding or assignment points. Life happens.
- **Quizzes are points-based with unlimited retakes — your best score counts.** For the *challenge*, the top 2 teams by average score each earn a point, so there's extra incentive to keep trying.
- **Fun challenges are worth 2 points each** and are group submissions. One person submits on behalf of the team, but everyone should contribute. These are designed to be short (30 minutes or less) and fun.

---

## Fun Challenges

Each week, a short challenge will be posted on Canvas. These are designed to reinforce what you're learning in a low-stakes, creative way. Formats include:

- Predicting what code will produce
- Finding bugs in broken code
- Answering questions about a dataset
- Redesigning ugly plots
- R trivia

Challenges are posted each week and due the following **Sunday at 11:59 PM**. One team member submits on Canvas for the group.

**No late submissions** on fun challenges. The 48-hour grace period that applies to assignments and quizzes does not apply here. Miss the deadline, miss the points.

---

## The Scoreboard

Every Monday (or the first class of the week), we'll show the current standings. Teams are ranked by total points across all four categories. You'll get to see how your team stacks up --- and who's gaining ground.

---

## The Power Play (Week 7)

Two-thirds of the way through the term, things get interesting. After the Week 7 scoreboard is revealed, the **top 2 teams** each get a one-time strategic choice:

- **Boost:** Add **+3 points** to your own team's total
- **Sabotage:** Remove **2 points** from each of **two other teams** of your choice

Choices are announced live in class. Choose wisely.

---

## FAQ

**What if someone drops the course?**
Your team won't be penalized. Pair coding and assignment points still use the "all but one" threshold, and averages adjust automatically.

**What if there's a tie at the end?**
If two or more teams are tied for first place, they all win. More pizza for everyone.

**Can I switch teams?**
Teams are set for the term. Part of the challenge is learning to work with your teammates.

**Do I have to participate?**
The challenge doesn't affect your grade, but everything that earns challenge points (pair coding, assignments, quizzes) is already part of your coursework. The fun challenges are the only extra thing, and they're short.
